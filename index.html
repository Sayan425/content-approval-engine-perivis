SECTION 04

17 Content Types: Full Playbooks

Each type is broken down into format, hook approach, structure, example, frequency, and the job it does for the strategy.

TYPE 01 · SIGNAL STORIES

Signal Stories

Take a raw market signal — a filing, an executive quote, a regulatory disclosure, a fund flow number — and narrate why it matters. The structure moves from raw data to interpretation to implication. This is the flagship content type for Perivis. It demonstrates the product value directly in the post.

Format
Text + data screenshot / infographic card (60%); Text-only with embedded numbers (40%). Carousels for multi-step signals.
Structure
  • Signal (1 line, specific)
  • Context (Why now?)
  • What most people missed
  • The real implication
  • Open question to audience
Pillar
Signal Storytelling

Job: Position CEO as the first-mover intelligence layer. Shows the product's value without demoing it.
Example Hook HDFC AMC just moved ₹4,200 crore into short-duration debt in 72 hours.
The media is calling it "defensive allocation."
It isn't. Here's what the filing actually shows → [thread]
TYPE 02 · BUILD IN PUBLIC

Document Perivis Updates

Transparent, behind-the-curtain posts about building Perivis — what was shipped, what failed, a key product decision, a user insight that changed the roadmap. This is not a product announcement. It's a founder's log. It humanizes the brand and builds parasocial investment in the product's success.

Format
Text + screenshot (product UI, dashboard, metric). Authentic and unpolished. Not a press release. Think: founder's notebook page.
Structure
  • What we shipped / what broke
  • The insight that led there
  • What we learned
  • What's next
Pillar
Product & Memes

Job: Build audience loyalty and product waitlist pipeline through founder authenticity.
Example Hook We analyzed 2,300 Perivis signals last month.
Only 6% of them made it into a mainstream headline within 48 hours.
The other 94%? Here's where they went.
TYPE 03 · TEARDOWN / BREAKDOWN

Tear Down / Break Down Something

Pick a company, a market event, a regulatory document, a fund's portfolio, or a CEO's statement — and systematically disassemble it into intelligence components. This is the "long-form" content type that builds deepest credibility. Think of it as showing the work that goes into building Perivis's signals.

Format
Multi-page document / long-scroll carousel (8–12 slides). Or rich text with multiple data points. High image/table density.
Structure
  • Entity / Event (what we're tearing down)
  • Surface reading (what everyone says)
  • Layer 1: The data beneath
  • Layer 2: The structural dynamic
  • Layer 3: Second-order effect
  • Verdict: What this really means
Pillar
Teardowns & Frameworks

Job: Evergreen saves + shares. Positions Perivis CEO as the most analytical voice in the Indian capital markets space.
Example Hook Zomato's Q3 result: ₹351 crore PAT.
Everyone's celebrating the profit.
I ran it through 6 signals.
The real story is hiding in the unit economics → [breakdown]
TYPE 04 · 1 SIGNAL = 5 IMPLICATIONS

1 Datapoint / Signal → 5 Implications

Take one hard, specific number and fan out its implications across five connected domains. This format is LinkedIn gold — it is structured, scannable, surprising at each step, and demonstrates analytical depth. It directly mirrors what Perivis does as a product: turning one raw signal into structured intelligence.

Format
Text-heavy numbered list (5 points). Or carousel with one implication per slide. Each implication should surprise, connect to something non-obvious.
Structure
  • Signal: [The one data point]
  • Implication 1: [Industry X]
  • Implication 2: [Policy/Regulatory]
  • Implication 3: [Investor behavior]
  • Implication 4: [Competitor/Market]
  • Implication 5: [Non-obvious tail]
Pillar
Signal Storytelling

Job: The highest-density format for demonstrating analytical value. Strong save rate = algorithmic boost.
Example Hook India's 10-year bond yield just crossed 7.1%.
One number. Five things nobody's talking about. 🧵

1/ MF debt allocations will shift in Q4...
2/ Real estate cap rates are about to get repriced...
TYPE 05 · CONTRARIAN TAKES

Contrarian Takes on the Markets

Deliberately pick a mainstream market narrative and tear it apart with data and logic. This is the highest-engagement format in the benchmarking set — Nithin Kamath's ~2,200 average like count is built almost entirely on contrarian truth-telling. Perivis has the data advantage to do this credibly.

Format
Text + chart/data image. Strong opening assertion. Never hedge in the first 3 lines. The nuance comes later, after the hook has landed.
Structure
  • Bold contrarian assertion (1–2 lines)
  • The mainstream narrative (steelman it)
  • The data that breaks it
  • The actual dynamic at play
  • What to actually watch instead
Pillar
Market Intelligence

Job: High-impression, high-debate content. Comments drive distribution. The controversy IS the algorithm.
Example Hook "FII outflows are killing the market."
Wrong framing. Wrong data. Wrong conclusion.
Here's what's actually happening →
TYPE 16 · HISTORICAL STORIES

Historical Stories (2nd Order Thinking)

Go beyond the surface of economic history. Instead of just telling what happened, focus on the ripples — the second and third-order consequences of a collapse or a boom. This positions the CEO as a deep thinker who understands the systemic complexity of markets, not just the headlines.

Format
Long-form text story (300+ words) or a detailed carousel. Rich in historical context but focused on the 'Thinking' framework.
Structure
  • The Event (The Peso Collapse, etc.)
  • 1st Order Effect (The obvious impact)
  • 2nd Order Effect (The hidden reaction)
  • 3rd Order Effect (The long-term shift)
  • Modern Parallel: What to watch today
Pillar
Market Intelligence

Job: Build intellectual authority. Demonstrates the depth of 'System Thinking' that powers the Perivis platform.
Example Hook When the Mexican Peso collapsed in 1994, the world watched the currency.
But the real story wasn't the exchange rate.
It was the 2nd order effect on global debt markets that changed finance forever.
TYPE 17 · EVERYTHING IS CONNECTED

Everything is Connected (Causal Chains)

The 'Butterfly Effect' for capital markets. Connect two seemingly unrelated events across time or geography to show how the world is a giant web of signals. This is high-engagement 'Mind-Blown' content that gets shared because it makes the reader feel smarter for knowing the connection.

Format
Visual connectivity map (infographic) or a fast-paced 'If-Then' text post. Needs to feel like a mystery being solved.
Structure
  • The Hook: The shocking connection (Point A → Point Z)
  • The Chain: A → B → C → D
  • The Climax: How D led to Z
  • The Signal: How to spot these chains today
Pillar
Market Intelligence

Job: Massive virality and shareability. Positions Perivis as the 'Global Brain' for market signals.
Example Hook How a famine in Bengal led to the American Civil War.
It sounds like a stretch.
But if you follow the capital flows and the commodity signals, the chain is unbreakable. 🧵

Hook Frameworks: The First 2 Lines

On LinkedIn, the hook is everything — it determines whether the "see more" gets clicked. Based on benchmarking analysis of the top-performing posts across all 7 competitors, six hook frameworks work for this audience.

Framework Best For Sample Templates Usage Strategy
H-01 The Exposed Assumption Contrarian, Debunk "Everyone says [X]. The data says [Y]."
"[Common belief] is costing investors [loss]." 		State a mainstream narrative contradiction in line 1, back it with Perivis data in line 2.
H-02 The Specific Number Drop Signal Stories, Smart Money "₹[X] crore moved in [timeframe]. Nobody noticed."

SECTION 06

Format Strategy & Production Mix

No video. Every other format is in play. Here's the recommended split and what each format is optimized to do.

55% Text + Image / Chart
15% Carousels / Docs
15% Text Only
15% Memes / Graphics
Format A · 55% of mix

Text + Image / Chart

The most versatile format. A strong text post paired with a data screenshot, chart, or branded data card. Fast to produce, high credibility, and performs across all content types. The image carries the proof; the text carries the narrative.

Format B · 15% of mix

Carousels & Documents

Groww's highest-performing content is carousels. Pranjal Kamra's most-saved content is structured carousels. Use for: Education Frameworks, Cheat Sheets, Teardowns. LinkedIn rewards time-spent on content.
